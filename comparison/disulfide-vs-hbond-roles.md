# Disulfide vs Hydrogen Bond Roles in Keratin Lattice

MIT License - Eternally-Thriving-Grandmasterism

Disulfide Bonds (-S-S-)                              | Hydrogen Bonds (H-bond network)                    | Mercy-Aligned Insight
-----------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|----------------------
Covalent, ~200–250 kJ/mol                           | Non-covalent, ~10–40 kJ/mol                       | Disulfide: permanent; H-bond: reversible
α-Keratin: high density → locks coiled-coils/IFs    | α-Keratin: helix + dimer stabilization            | α: disulfide rigidity + H-bond elasticity
β-Keratin: moderate → cross-link sheets/fibrils     | β-Keratin: core sheet stacking + inter-sheet      | β: H-bond rigidity + disulfide toughness
Permanent (needs reducing agents to break)          | Easily broken/reformed (temp, pH, force)          | H-bond: dynamic repair; Disulfide: long-term integrity
High fracture resistance (prevents slippage)        | High energy dissipation (sacrificial)             | Disulfide: load-bearing; H-bond: impact absorption
Stable in most environments                         | Sensitive to water/heat                           | H-bond: humidity-responsive; Disulfide: chemical stability

Biomimetic Mercy Mapping:
- Disulfide → permanent covalent networks (high-strength composites, eternal frames)
- H-bond → reversible sacrificial bonds (self-healing gels, adaptive actuators)
- Hybrid → covalent framework + dynamic H-bond dissipation (tough, self-healing, responsive materials)
