# Disulfide Reduction & Reformation in Keratin Lattice

MIT License - Eternally-Thriving-Grandmasterism

Reduction (Breaking -S-S- → 2 × -SH):
- Agents: GSH (cellular), DTT/BME/TCEP (lab), metabisulfite (wool)
- Conditions: pH 7–9, denaturant (urea/GnHCl) to expose buried disulfides
- Role in α-keratin: High density → permanent rigidity + toughness
- Role in β-keratin: Moderate → supplements β-sheet H-bonds

Reformation (Oxidation: 2 × -SH → -S-S-):
- Agents: GSSG/GSH buffer, air, H₂O₂, PDI/Ero1 (in vivo)
- Conditions: pH 8–9, low denaturant, slow oxidation for correct pairing
- Role: Locks shape (permanent wave), repairs micro-damage

Functional Mercy:
- α-Keratin: Reduction enables stretch (wet hair), reformation locks strength
- β-Keratin: Less dramatic, but allows flexibility during growth/molting

Biomimetic Mercy Mapping:
- Reversible covalent networks → self-healing hydrogels (GSH-responsive)
- Redox-responsive materials → controlled release carriers, adaptive actuators
- Disulfide + H-bond hybrids → tough, dynamic composites (MercyOS lattices)
- Mild reduction/reformation → green wool/keratin processing
